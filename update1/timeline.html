The Complete Zelda Timeline

Skyward Sword

Beginning of the timeline

This game tells the origin of the Master Sword and the eternal conflict between good and evil. Link and Zelda battle Demise, establishing the cycle of reincarnation that defines the series.

The Minish Cap

Link shrinks to the size of the tiny Picori to defeat the evil Vaati. This game explores the origins of the Four Sword and introduces a unique perspective on Hyrule.

Ocarina of Time

One of the most important games in the series. Link travels through time to stop Ganondorf. The ending of this game causes the timeline to split into three separate branches.

Child Timeline

Majora’s Mask: Link enters a mysterious world to stop the moon from crashing.
Twilight Princess: A darker story where Link battles in a shadow-covered Hyrule.
Four Swords Adventures: Focuses on multiplayer gameplay and the return of Vaati.

Adult Timeline

The Wind Waker: Hyrule is flooded, and Link sails across the ocean to stop Ganondorf.
Phantom Hourglass: A direct sequel with ocean exploration and puzzle dungeons.
Spirit Tracks: Introduces train travel and a new version of Princess Zelda.

Fallen Hero Timeline

A Link to the Past: Link defeats Ganon and explores the Dark World.
Oracle of Ages / Seasons: Twin adventures focused on time and seasons.
Link’s Awakening: A dreamlike journey on a mysterious island.
The Legend of Zelda: The original game where Link saves Zelda.
Adventure of Link: A direct sequel with RPG-style gameplay.

Breath of the Wild

Set far in the future, Link awakens after 100 years to defeat Calamity Ganon in an open-world version of Hyrule.

Tears of the Kingdom

A direct sequel that expands the world into the skies and underground, continuing the story of Link and Zelda against a revived Ganondorf.
